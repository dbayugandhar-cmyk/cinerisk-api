# CINEOS Legal Position — Data Collection & Attribution

**Date:** May 2026 | **Contact:** yugandhar@cineos.in
**Patent:** US Provisional Patent 64/049,190

## 1. Nature of Data Collected

CINEOS collects **only publicly accessible data** from:
- Public Telegram channels (not private groups or DMs)
- IndiaMART public seller listings
- Meesho public product listings
- Public Instagram, YouTube, Twitter/X posts
- Public ShareChat posts and Discord servers
- Web-indexed content via search engines

**CINEOS does NOT collect:**
- Private messages of any kind
- Personal data of private individuals not engaged in fraud
- Financial account details of private persons
- Aadhaar, PAN or government ID of private persons

## 2. Legal Basis

### IT Act 2000 and DPDP Act 2023
- Collection limited to publicly available information
- No sensitive personal data collected
- Fraud detection constitutes legitimate interest
- Intelligence for law enforcement = public interest purpose

### IT Act 2000 Section 65B Compliance
Every finding is SHA-256 hashed, HMAC-SHA256 signed and
timestamped to millisecond precision. This meets Section 65B
requirements for admissibility in police FIRs, SEBI SCORES
complaints, GST Council reports and civil proceedings.

## 3. Attribution Methodology

### What CINEOS attributes (all from public sources):
- Phone numbers publicly posted by operators in channels
- UPI IDs publicly posted by operators in channels
- Channel usernames (public by definition)
- Network affiliations (public channel cross-links)

### What CINEOS does NOT do:
- Access private data without legal authority
- Conduct surveillance on private individuals
- Make arrests or enforcement decisions
- Replace law enforcement — supplements it

### Confidence scoring:
- 95%: Phone publicly posted + multi-channel confirmation
- 80%: Channel name pattern + network links confirmed
- 50%: Keyword match only — flagged for review

## 4. False Positive Management

Multi-signal verification required before any finding:
1. Explicit keyword match (e.g. "first copy", "replica")
2. Price anomaly vs retail price
3. Platform signal corroboration
4. Network affiliation confirmation

Documented accuracy rate: see reports/accuracy_audit.json
